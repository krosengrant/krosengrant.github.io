Case Study

Quick Lit

A short story reader app

Date Spring 2019
My Role Designer, Researcher
Tools Adobe Xd, Notion, Ikonate, Undraw

The Problem

I noticed a pattern. People wanted to read more but were having trouble focusing or finding the energy to tackle traditional books after a long day. Reading for a few minutes a day, in the form of short stories, could help people get back into the habit of reading.


Research

Reading should be a leisure activity, so why does it feel like work? I wanted to determine what was standing between people and their reading goals. To do this, I pulled relevant quotes from discussions on Reddit's r/books.

These relevant discussions revolved around short stories, different mediums (electronic vs. paper), audiobooks and reasons for reading or not reading. I then added tags for "reasons for reading" and "reasons for not reading". Combined with existing research on reading habits, I created a persona to describe the anticipated typical user.

I tagged each quote to determine why people were not reading even though they wanted to. I consolidated these reasons into a list of user needs to determine what features should be included.

I also researched competing mobile apps to determine which features would distinguish Quick Lit. To synthesize this research, I created a list of features using the "MoSCoW" method to list the must have, should have, could have and won't have features.

User Testing

Based on low and high fidelity prototype testing, the user enjoyed the "[X] minute read" estimate feature, the "Welcome back, [name]" next to the resume story button at the top of the app's home page, and the ability to resume reading from the tab bar menu.

Some areas of weakness and future improvements include

  • Difficulty locating settings icon → Replace adjustment slider icon with gear icon
  • Difficulty noticing tab bar initially → Page cover preview may make tab bar blend in with rest of feed. Try adding color to tab bar.
  • Confusion regarding turning on "night mode" (black background with white text) → Consolidate "color" and "automatic nightmode" options into one category

Project Retrospective

I would like to perform more user testing to determine which other features users expect in the app beyond the baseline features I included.
